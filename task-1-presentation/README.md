##### This infographic serves as a comprehensive visual guide to digital literacy for students, presented in a bold dark-themed design with neon accents. It is organized into four key sections.

##### 

##### The first section defines digital literacy as the ability to find, evaluate, create, and communicate information digitally — built on four pillars: Find, Protect, Create, and Connect. The second section covers safe internet practices, highlighting four essential rules — using strong passwords with 2FA, avoiding phishing scams, accessing only HTTPS sites, and thinking before clicking.

##### 

##### The third section introduces digital tools for students, recommending platforms like Notion, Canva, Google Scholar, and Trello for productivity and research. Finally, the email etiquette section demonstrates professional communication through a sample email structure and five golden rules — from writing clear subject lines to signing off formally.

##### 

##### Together, these sections equip students with the foundational skills to navigate the digital world safely, smartly, and professionally.
